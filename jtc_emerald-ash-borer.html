The Emerald Ash Borer

The Agrilus Planipennis, better known as the Emerald Ash Borer are an exotic beetle from asia. They were first found in america in southeastern Michigan in 2002, they were most likely brought here on solid wood packing materials from its native land of asia. As of August 2017, the Emerald Ash Borer can be found in 31 states and portions of canadian cities. The Ash Borer and its larvae feed on ash foliage, this eventually disrupts the trees ability to carry water and nutrients inside the tree.




  • The Ash Borers have killed hundreds of millions of ash trees in north america alone.
  • Cost municipalities, property owners, nursery operators and forest products industries hundreds of millions of dollars.
  • Caused regulatory agencies and the USDA to enforce quarantines and fines to prevent potentially infested ash trees,
    logs or hardwood firewood from moving out of areas where EAB occurs.
